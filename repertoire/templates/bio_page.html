Musical Director, Conductor, Educator

Bam!

It’s one of Liza’s favorite words. And totally representative of the impact she makes on her orchestras and her audiences. Her energy is radiant and infectious and she gives it freely to every student in her orchestras. She is the founding Music Director of the Contemporary Youth Orchestra, conducting faculty at Interlochen Center for the Arts and co-founder of the Interlochen Arts Camp Junior Advanced String Institute. She also serves as guest conductor for youth orchestras across the country.

Boots

Liza often wears fabulous rocker boots when she’s on the podium conducting her musicians, especially during her Rock the Orchestra series with rock stars like of Styx, Jefferson Starship, Ray Manzarek, Graham Nash, Jon Anderson and Pat Benatar. And she has worked some pretty big stages.

Stages

Blossom Music Center. Severance Hall. Nautica Stage. Interlochen. Liza has conducted major artists performing major works on these stages and more, including 18 world-premiere concertos performed by members of the Cleveland Orchestra, nationally recognized musicians and prominent jazz artists. She has conducted more than 90 orchestral world premieres, all with the composers present, including Bernard Rands, Robert Ward and Joan Tower.

Reviews

STRAD magazine called Liza “a master of tutelage...” That’s an understatement. Her student musicians are the real beneficiaries of her skill with the stick and the score. They don’t just get excited to perform. They learn; they grow; they flourish.
